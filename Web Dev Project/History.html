A Brief History of Clay Target Shooting

Clay target shooting is actually a quite recent invention. Before the inception of the modern clay target, shotgun shooting sports would involve live animals, making it more of a luxury than a sport that anyone could join. However, once the clay target was invented and began to be used in earnest, the sport quickly caught on, and by the mid 1900's, the sports that are known and played today were created.

Below is a short timeline of the major events across the short history of the sport. Simply click on a date if you would like to know more about it.

1860's
First Use of Artificial Targets

Before clay targets were invented, the first artifical targets were instead simple glass balls. A mechanical trap was used to launch them into the air for shooting.

1882
First True Clay Targets

The first breakable clay targets were invented in the USA by George Ligowsky.

Described as a saucer-shaped pice of bittle crockery. These discs were launched by a springboard trap.

1888
Modern Clay Targets invented

Clay targets were now being made with lime and pitch, the same way that they are created today.

1900
Olympics introduces Shooting

The Olympics offically introduced clay target shooting to their lineup of games. The sport was then know as Olympic Trap.

1921
Use of Live Game Banned

In the United Kingdom, this marked an end of an era, where using live game in shooting sports was outlawed.

1923
Game of Skeet invented

In this year, the modern variation of skeet was created by William Harnden Foster. The game was orignally played in a 360 degree circle, but was cut down into just a half circle. In addition, William added a second trap house, making a total of two.

1950
Olympic Trap Introduced

The modern variation of Olympic Trap was introduced to the Olympics on this year. Olympic Trap uses multitude of traps that randomly fire, as oppposed to normal trap, that simply uses one machine.

1968
Olympic Skeet Introduced

On this year, Olymic Skeet was introduced to the Olympics. It plays much like the regular game of skeet, but is more regulated, and the dics are thrown at a much faster speed.

1980's
Automatic Clay Throwers Introduced

In the 80's the use and manufacturing of automatic clay throwers began to grow. This would help grow the sport much larger in size, as it allowed more people to own and operate them.

1996
Olympic Shooting Divided by Gender

This year marked the first Olympic Games where shooting was seperated by gender. Before this point, men and women would compete against each other for the medal.

TODAY
Modern Day

Today, Clay Target Shooting is enjoyed by a many number of people, old and young. Currently there are a number of high schools introducing the sport to younger people, and the sport continues to grow. Currently, three different games are in the Olympics: Olympic Skeet, Olympic Trap, and Olympic Double Trap.
