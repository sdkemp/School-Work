Clay Target Shooting and the Olympics

Quickly after Clay Target Shooting began as a sport, it was introduced to the Olympic Games. In the year 1900, at the second ever Olympic Games, trap was brought on as the first ever shotgun sport to be added. Throughout the majority of all modern Olympic Games there has been at least one shotgun sport included. While skeet was only introduced in 1968, since then, both skeet and trap have been included in the games every year. Up until 1996, the games were not split into genders, and instead, both man and woman alike competed for the gold, but after that year, the games have since been divided like so.
