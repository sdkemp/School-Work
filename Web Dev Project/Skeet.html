Skeet Shooting

One of the many sports in Clay Target Shooting, is skeet shooting. It invovles a field with 8 different stations, and two separate target houses - a high house and a low house.

If you would like to know more, an interactible field is available below, just click on a section to expand it.

A skeet field is comprised of two different houses, a low house and a high house. Each house contains a clay target thrower, and upon button press, will launch a clay target towards the center of the field.

A game of skeet consists of rounds, with each round being a total of 25 shots. The goal is to hit all 25 shots across the field.

Each player rotates through all of the 8 stations, with each station group requiring them to make a different amount of shots.

Each station requires the shooter to make a minimum of two shots, one from the high house, and one from the low.

Furthur information about each station is given below.

A player begins a round at these two stations. On each of these stations, the player makes 4 shots. The first two are made in a "doubles" fashion. This means that both houses throw a clay at the same time, and the play must shoot both. The second set of shots are made singularly, with the player calling for each house indivudually.

Stations 6 and 7 are also doubles stations, and the player repeats what they did on stations 1 and 2.

For stations 3, 4, 5, and 6, the player makes only two shots each at these stations. They will call for each house, one at a time, before moving on to the next station.

Station 8 is a unique station. It plays like the other singles stations, but instead the player is trying to hit the clays as they are coming towards them.
